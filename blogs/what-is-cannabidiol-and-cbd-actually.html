What is Cannabidiol and CBD actually?

Author: D. Astor

Published: April 20, 2020

Cannabinoid contained within the actual cannabis plant material, it is extracted in a multistep manufacturing process, and various types of cannabidiol are available to consumers and referred to as CBD.

People can consume CBD products by ingesting them, inhaling, or utilizing it as a topical - transdermal product.

CBD topicals are becoming increasingly popular, you can find a wide selection which come in various forms such as; balms, salves, lotions, serums, soaks, sprays, patches, etc.

CBD topicals are seen across the board in several categories including; beauty products, cosmetics, bath, body, pain, over the counter relief, spa treatment products, skincare, facial masks, you name it someone has made it.

The average cost for a product ranges from a single item priced at $7.99 for a less than 1-ounce product containing 2 - 10 milligrams of CBD, and higher milligrams and fluid ounce products averaging 100-1,000 milligrams of CBD priced for as much as $300 to $500.

The cost for a quality CBD product is based upon the going rate of the ingredient and it has fluctuated due to the amount of access the U.S. now has to new products being grown domestically.

What should you look for when shopping for CBD? Try to find items described as full-spectrum which are made with 100% U.S. grown hemp. It is also to your advantage to research the company's third-party lab results. You must also consider the other ingredients contained within the product which have an increased impact on the effectiveness.

The better the ingredient, the higher the price. Try to avoid products that are made and packaged in other countries due to the lower standards and regulations placed upon them. The U.S. still has very high consumer product manufacturing and distribution standards.
